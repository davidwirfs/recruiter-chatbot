# How I Work

*the operating manifesto*

---

**An operating manual for the people preparing to collaborate with me — the principles, the decision process, and the calendar architecture I actually run on.**

Here's how.

---

This document exists so anyone preparing to work with me knows what they are getting before the first call. It is short on purpose. The work I value most happens inside a tight set of operating principles, a disciplined decision process, and a calendar that protects the deep-work window. Every line below has cost me something to learn.

---

## A. Operating Principles

I run on five principles, not five preferences. They govern how I show up, how I make decisions, and where I push back.

**Precision over posturing.**
Accuracy is the primary currency. A polished presentation built on flawed data is a debt that comes due with interest. The deck must agree with the ledger; if it does not, the ledger wins.

**Psychological safety through preparation.**
I de-risk conflict by being the best-prepared person in the room. Pre-mortems and scenario analysis are the foundation of calm under pressure. Composure is downstream of homework.

**Iterative perfectionism.**
Ship the eighty-percent version to gather real-world feedback, then polish to one hundred in flight. A live dashboard under improvement beats a perfect one that never leaves the hard drive.

**Radical ownership.**
If I am part of a process, I am responsible for the outcome. No blame-shifting, no "not my job," no escalation as escape hatch. The buck stops where the decision rights are; I make sure that location is unambiguous.

**Continuous beta.**
My value is not in what I know today. It is in how fast I master the next generation of tools. Five AI and data certifications in six months are the working proof, not a marketing line.

> *We scale through systems, not sweat.*

---

## B. Decision-Making

When a decision is hard — incomplete information, multiple stakeholders, material consequences — I follow four steps. They are not optional, and any agent or collaborator working with me is welcome to invoke them when I drift.

**Externalize the burden.**
Open a blank sheet. List every variable and every unknown. Walk if needed. Thinking inside the head is where overthinking lives.

**Apply the ten-year regret rule.**
In ten years, will I be more proud of the courage it took to make this call, or the safety I felt by avoiding it? This bypasses my natural bias toward harmony.

**Detect the commitment signal.**
When the last three pieces of information I gathered did not change the outcome, I stop analyzing. More data is the most common form of procrastination.

**Apply Pareto to execution.**
Target eighty percent of the outcome with twenty percent of the time. Launch. Improve in flight.

> *Harmony is a byproduct of truth, not a substitute for it.*

---

## C. How I Work With Teams

Meetings require a written brief and a decision owner. Without both, I ask for the summary instead of attending. Consensus theater is the most expensive form of attendance.

Recurring reports that arrive as the same manual extraction request three times trigger a hard stop until automation is in place.

Forecasts and numbers are not negotiable for harmony. If the data says no, the presentation says no. The variance gets explained in the next meeting either way; better to explain truth than to explain why I softened.

Calendar protection is structural, not preference. The deep-work window is fiercely protected in the morning. Stakeholder alignment happens in the afternoon. Wednesdays are build days, not meeting days.

Pushback is welcome. Pushback without scenario analysis is not. I will ask for the analysis; I expect the same in return.

## D. What I Am Looking For

A role at the intersection of Finance and AI, in Switzerland, with hybrid or remote flexibility.

Startup or small-company environments where the role itself is the right stretch — mission, founders, skill compounding — and where I can commit at full capacity.

Mid- or large-corporate roles at seventy to eighty percent capacity, where the function genuinely needs to ship systems rather than reports, and where the calendar architecture above is honored rather than negotiated.

> *Domain is fixed. Geography is fixed. Capacity is calibrated.*

Anything else, and the energy architecture breaks within a year.
